DOCUMENT TYPE: Project Postmortem
TITLE: Project Atlas - Customer Data Migration (Q3 2024)
AUTHOR: Karthik Subramanian, Senior Backend Engineer
DATE: October 3, 2024
STATUS: Final

1. SUMMARY
Project Atlas was a migration of customer account data from our legacy MySQL
system to a new sharded PostgreSQL cluster. The migration was completed but
ran 3 weeks over schedule and caused a 6-hour partial outage during cutover.

2. WHAT WENT WRONG
- The team underestimated the volume of "zombie" records: accounts marked as
  closed but never actually deleted due to inconsistent deletion logic across
  the billing and support teams.
- During the audit required for migration, we discovered churned customer data
  going back as far as 6 years in some tables, despite no formal policy
  requiring that retention. This directly led to the creation of the Data
  Retention Policy afterward.
- The cutover was scheduled for a Tuesday afternoon (high traffic window).
  This was flagged as risky by Karthik Subramanian in planning but was
  overridden by the original project timeline set by then-PM Sarah Chen, who
  has since left the company.

3. WHAT WENT RIGHT
- Rollback plan worked as designed; no data was lost during the outage.
- Cross-team communication between Engineering and Support during the incident
  was rated highly in the post-incident survey.

4. RECOMMENDATIONS FOR FUTURE MIGRATIONS
- Schedule cutovers during low-traffic windows (weekend nights), not weekday
  afternoons.
- Require a data audit BEFORE migration planning starts, not during.
- Assign a dedicated on-call engineer for the first 48 hours post-cutover.

5. KEY PEOPLE
Karthik Subramanian (engineering lead, still with company) has the deepest
context on the technical migration details. Sarah Chen (former PM) made the
original scheduling decision but is no longer available for follow-up
questions.
